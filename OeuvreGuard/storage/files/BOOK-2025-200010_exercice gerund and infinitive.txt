Use the Gerund (-ing form) after:
1. Certain verbs:
Some verbs are always followed by a gerund.

Examples:

enjoy → I enjoy reading.

avoid → He avoided talking to her.

suggest → She suggested going out.

Common verbs followed by gerund:

enjoy, avoid, suggest, keep, admit, deny, mind, finish, consider, practice, miss, postpone, risk

2. Prepositions (always followed by -ing):
Examples:

She’s good at drawing.

They left without saying goodbye.

I’m interested in learning Spanish.

3. Phrasal verbs:
Examples:

I gave up smoking.

They ended up moving to Paris.

4. As the subject of a sentence:
Example:

Swimming is good exercise.

Learning languages takes time.

✅ Use the Infinitive (to + verb) after:
1. Certain verbs:
Some verbs are followed by the infinitive.

Examples:

decide → She decided to go.

hope → I hope to see you soon.

promise → He promised to help.

Common verbs followed by infinitive:

want, need, would like, decide, learn, agree, hope, offer, plan, refuse, try, promise, expect

2. Adjectives:
Examples:

It's easy to learn.

I'm happy to help.

She’s ready to start.

3. Purpose or intention:
Example:

I came here to study.

He works hard to earn money.

⚠️ Some verbs can take both, but the meaning changes:
1. Remember, forget, stop, try
I remember meeting her. (I met her in the past — memory)

I remember to call her. (I won’t forget to do it — future action)

She stopped smoking. (She quit smoking.)

She stopped to smoke. (She stopped doing something else in order to smoke.)
